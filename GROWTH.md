# Growth & Marketing Strategy

## Quick Wins (Week 1)

### 1. Get Followers Quickly
- **Local Reddit**: Post to r/Chattanooga with "I built a daily voting game for Chattanooga - which taco spot is better?"
- **Facebook Groups**: Join Chattanooga community groups, post matchups as conversation starters
- **Instagram**: Create @scrumblechatt account, post matchup results as stories/reels with local tags
- **Twitter/X**: Tweet at local influencers, news outlets (@noogatoday, @timesfreepress)
- **LinkedIn**: Post in Chattanooga professional groups about the mayor/business matchups

### 2. Get Beta Testers (First 100 Users)
- **Friends & Family**: Text 20 people the link, ask them to vote and share
- **Local Slack/Discord**: Post in Chattanooga tech/startup communities
- **Coffee Shop Flyers**: Print QR codes, post at Velo, Frothy Monkey, Mean Mug
- **Email Signature**: Add "Vote on today's Chattanooga matchup: scrumble.chat"
- **Nextdoor**: Post as "local project" asking for feedback

### 3. Marketing Tactics
**Content Marketing**
- Daily Instagram story: "Today's winner: [X] beat [Y] 234-189"
- Weekly recap email: "This week's champions"
- Controversial matchups: "Is [X] really better than [Y]? Vote now"

**Viral Mechanics**
- Add "Share your vote" button that pre-fills social posts
- Show "You're in the majority/minority" after voting
- Leaderboard: "Most voted matchup this week"
- Streak counter: "You've voted 7 days in a row!"

**Partnerships**
- Contact losing businesses: "You lost to [X] - want a rematch?"
- Contact winning businesses: "You won! Share this badge"
- Local news: Pitch weekly "Chattanooga Favorites" segment
- Tourism board: "See what locals really think"

**Paid Ads (Budget: $100-500/mo)**
- Facebook Ads: Target Chattanooga, ages 25-45, interests: local food/politics
- Instagram Ads: Carousel of matchup results
- Google Ads: "Chattanooga voting game", "best tacos Chattanooga"

## Growth Loops

### Loop 1: Viral Voting
1. User votes on matchup
2. Sees surprising result
3. Shares "Can't believe [X] beat [Y]!"
4. Friends click, vote, repeat

### Loop 2: Business Engagement
1. Business loses matchup
2. Owner shares to rally supporters
3. Supporters vote in next matchup
4. More businesses want to participate

### Loop 3: Content Flywheel
1. Matchup generates discussion
2. Discussion becomes content (blog post, social post)
3. Content ranks in Google
4. New users discover via search
5. More votes = more data = more content

## Monetization Ideas (Future)

1. **Sponsored Matchups**: Businesses pay to be featured ($50-200/matchup)
2. **Premium Listings**: Enhanced profiles for businesses ($20/mo)
3. **Data Reports**: Sell aggregate voting data to businesses ($100/report)
4. **Events**: Host live voting events at bars/venues ($500/event)
5. **Franchise**: License to other cities ($1000/city/year)

## Competitive Advantages

- **Local focus**: Not competing with Yelp/Google, complementing them
- **Daily habit**: New matchup every day = reason to return
- **Controversial**: Generates discussion and shares
- **Simple**: One click to vote, no signup required
- **Fun**: Gamified, not serious reviews

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Don't spam local groups (post max 1x/week)
- Don't fake votes (kills trust)
- Don't ignore negative feedback
- Don't over-monetize early (focus on growth)
- Don't neglect mobile experience

## Success Milestones

- **100 votes**: Proof of concept
- **1,000 votes**: Product-market fit
- **10,000 votes**: Local traction
- **100,000 votes**: Regional expansion ready
- **1M votes**: National franchise potential
